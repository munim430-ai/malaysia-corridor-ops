Section 60K Employment Pass Malaysia: Complete Guide for Employers 2026

Last updated July 2026 | 8 min read

What is Section 60K?

Section 60K refers to a provision in Malaysia's Employment Act 1955 that allows employers to hire foreign workers with specific exemptions from certain provisions of the Act. In practice, Section 60K approval (also referred to as JTKSM approval through the ePP60K system) is a mandatory prerequisite before any Malaysian employer can apply for foreign worker quota through FWCMS eQuota.

Think of it as the "permission to hire" step — before you can ask for a quota, you need Section 60K approval proving that you have a legitimate need for foreign workers that cannot be filled by local Malaysian workers.

📌 KEY POINT: Section 60K approval is a documentation step, not the same as quota approval. You need Section 60K clearance FIRST, then you apply for quota through FWCMS eQuota. The two are sequential, not simultaneous.

The Legal Basis

Under Section 60K of the Employment Act 1955:

  • The Director General of Labour (JTKSM/KESUMA) can grant exemptions from specific provisions of the Act
  • These exemptions cover working hours, rest days, holidays, and termination benefits for foreign workers
  • The exemption is personal to the employer — it cannot be transferred or assigned
  • It has a specific validity period and must be renewed
  • Violation of Section 60K conditions can result in revocation and penalties

Application Process

  1. Register on ePP60K portal: Create employer account with SSM/ROC registration details
  2. Submit supporting documents:
    • Company SSM registration certificate
    • Business licence (if applicable)
    • Justification for foreign worker requirement
    • Local workforce composition report
    • Efforts to recruit local workers (advertisements, interviews)
  3. Processing: JTKSM/KESUMA reviews application (typical: 14-30 working days)
  4. Approval: Section 60K exemption letter issued digitally
  5. Proceed to quota: Use Section 60K approval as prerequisite for FWCMS eQuota application

How Section 60K Relates to the Closed Corridor

An important nuance: employers CAN obtain Section 60K approval and even FWCMS eQuota approval while the Bangladesh corridor is closed. The approval is not tied to a specific source country. However, deploying workers requires the bilateral labour corridor to be operational.

What this means in practice:

  • Start now: Get your Section 60K approval in place while the corridor is closed
  • Apply for quota: Move to FWCMS eQuota once Section 60K is approved
  • Be ready: When the corridor reopens, you deploy immediately instead of waiting months for approvals
  • Risk: Quota approvals may have expiry dates — factor timing into your planning

Common Reasons for Section 60K Rejection

❌ Insufficient local hiring effort

Employer cannot demonstrate that local workers were sought but unavailable.

❌ Incorrect sector classification

Applying under wrong sector (e.g., services instead of manufacturing) leads to rejection.

❌ Expired or invalid licences

Company registration or industry licence not current.

❌ Past compliance issues

Previous violations of employment laws or foreign worker regulations.

❌ Incomplete documentation

Missing supporting documents — most common cause of delays.

Post-Approval Obligations

Once you have Section 60K approval and quota, you must:

  • Maintain proper employment records for all foreign workers
  • Comply with exemption conditions — the approval may specify certain terms
  • Renew on time — Section 60K approval has a validity period, typically 1-3 years
  • Report changes — any change in business operations, address, or ownership must be notified
  • Await corridor reopening — for Bangladesh workers, deployment cannot proceed until the corridor is operational

Employer Checklist

Use this checklist to prepare your Section 60K application:

  • ✅ SSM registration (active, not dormant)
  • ✅ Business licence (current year)
  • ✅ Local workforce report (Form E/borang E)
  • ✅ Justification letter (why foreign workers are needed)
  • ✅ Advertisement proof (local hiring attempts)
  • ✅ Company profile (operations, premises photos)
  • ✅ Financial statements (to show business viability)
  • ✅ Previous foreign worker records (if applicable)
