Malaysia-Bangladesh Labour Corridor Reopening: Complete Timeline & Analysis

Last updated July 2026 | 12 min read

Executive Summary

The Malaysia-Bangladesh labour corridor has been closed since June 1, 2024 — now over 31 months and counting. Despite significant progress in bilateral relations and multiple high-level meetings, a formal labour MoU (Memorandum of Understanding) has not yet been signed. This article provides a complete timeline of events, analysis of the key issues, and three scenarios for when the corridor might reopen.

🎯 KEY BOTTOM LINE: The most likely reopening window is late 2026 to early 2027, contingent on a signed labour MoU between Bangladesh and Malaysia. Pre-positioning quota through FWCMS eQuota now is the single most important step employers can take.

Complete Timeline

2024: The Closure Year

May 2024Malaysia announces suspension of Bangladesh labour recruitment. Immediate freeze on new applications.
1 Jun 2024Corridor officially closed. No new worker intake from Bangladesh. Thousands of approved quotas frozen.
Jun-Dec 2024Backchannel discussions begin. Malaysia expresses concerns over recruitment syndicates, document fraud, and worker welfare issues in the existing agent model.

Apr 2025Bangladesh government takes action against syndicate agents, clearing out known bad actors from the system.
H2 2025Both sides work on framework for new recruitment model. Discussions center on zero-fee, digital systems, and employer-pays principles.

2026: The Breakthrough Year

Jun 202622 Jun 2026PM Tarique Rahman visits KL: Cultural MoU signed, labour MoU NOT signed. PM requests accelerated corridor reopening.

Malaysia's 10 Conditions for Agency Re-accreditation

Malaysia has proposed 10 stringent conditions for Bangladesh recruiting agencies to qualify under the new system:

  1. Minimum 3,000 workers deployed annually — eliminates small, non-serious agencies
  2. 5+ years of continuous operation — proven track record required
  3. 10,000 sq ft office space — physical infrastructure requirement
  4. Training centre with certified curriculum — PDOT compliance
  5. Clean BMET compliance record — no violations in last 3 years
  6. Bank guarantee of BDT 50 lakh — financial solvency proof
  7. Dedicated welfare desk — in-country support for workers
  8. Digital tracking system — real-time worker status monitoring
  9. No sub-agent network — direct hiring only
  10. Compliance with Employer Pays Principle — zero-fee to workers

Impact: Only an estimated 5-7 agencies in Bangladesh can currently meet all 10 conditions. This means the post-reopening market will be highly concentrated among a few large, compliant players.

Employer Pays Principle: What It Means

Both governments agreed (April 2026) that the ILO Employer Pays Principle (EPP) will govern the new corridor. Under EPP:

  • Workers pay nothing for recruitment, documentation, medicals, training, or travel
  • Employers bear all recruitment costs — directly or through licensed agencies
  • No fee is charged to workers at any stage — before departure, during employment, or for contract renewal
  • Full cost transparency required — all fees must be disclosed and documented

This is a fundamental shift from the existing model where workers paid 6-7 lakh BDT through agent networks. While it protects workers, it also means employer costs per worker will rise — potentially by RM 5,000-8,000 per worker in upfront recruitment costs.

Key Takeaways for Employers

  1. Don't wait — apply for quota through FWCMS eQuota now. The 22,476 pending applications mean there's already a queue.
  2. Budget for EPP — the employer-pays model is coming. Factor RM 5,000-8,000 per worker in recruitment costs into your 2027 budget.
  3. Partner early — with only 5-7 agencies likely qualifying, secure partnerships with compliant agencies before the corridor opens.
  4. Compliance is not optional — CBP enforcement, RBA audits, and KESUMA's digital tracking mean undocumented recruitment is riskier than ever.

Bengali Workers: What This Means for You

If you're a Bangladeshi worker waiting to go to Malaysia: your patience will pay off. The new system, once operational, will be the most worker-friendly recruitment channel ever established between the two countries:

  • ✅ Zero recruitment fee — no more selling land or taking loans to pay agents
  • ✅ Full documentation — BMET clearance, proper contract, insurance
  • ✅ Digital tracking — you can verify your status at every step
  • ✅ Welfare support — in-country assistance upon arrival
