**Persona:** World-class investor

## Objection 13 — A previously-fixed compliance gap silently disappeared in the last redraft

**Probability of Rejection if Unresolved: 60%**

Redlining Round 2 against Round 1: Round 1's §10 was **Payment and Remittance Compliance** (Bangladesh Bank FEPD circulars, Authorized Dealer requirements for outward remittances). Round 2 renumbered sections to insert a new §10, **Data Protection & PDPA Compliance** — and the payment/remittance content was never carried forward under a new number. It simply vanished between drafts. This is exactly the kind of defect a diligence-minded investor finds by redlining plan versions, and its presence undermines confidence in the rest of the document's version control. **This must be restored as its own section, not merged/lost inside the new one** — the two compliance areas (data handling vs. money movement) are legally distinct and both real.

## Objection 14 — KESUMA's own stated stakeholder-engagement model runs through governments and incumbents, which makes the plan's "independent outreach" fallback close to a dead end

**Probability of Rejection if Unresolved: 55%**

Fresh reporting confirms KESUMA is conducting its foreign-worker-system review through "preliminary engagement sessions with labour source countries and industry stakeholders, including the technology provider [Bestinet/FWCMS]" — i.e., government-to-government channels and the existing incumbent platform operator, not open engagement with unaffiliated private ventures. Round 2 (§2.4) already concluded RL partnership likely gates regulatory access, but still budgeted "4-6 weeks of independent outreach" as a real parallel test. Given KESUMA's own described engagement model, that independent-outreach track should be treated as **very low probability of producing anything**, not a genuine fallback — the plan should stop hedging on it and commit resources to the RL-mediated and (where possible) BD-government-mediated paths from day one.

## Objection 15 — Every SME-targeting and pain-point claim is still secondary-sourced; there is no primary evidence a single real employer has been asked

**Probability of Rejection if Unresolved: 50%**

Three rounds of hardening have all relied on secondary sources — this repo's own prior research, an external AI-generated critique, and web reporting — to conclude that Malaysian SMEs are the right first target and are motivated by cost/efficiency pain. None of that is primary validation. An investor will ask: has anyone on this venture had an actual conversation with a real Malaysian SME employer, even an informal one? The plan should commit to obtaining 5-10 informal conversations or expressions of interest *before* or alongside the RL/ministry work — cheap, fast, and the single best way to de-risk the entire targeting thesis with real signal instead of inference.

## Objection 16 — The illustrative revenue model is never reconciled against this plan's own new costs

**Probability of Rejection if Unresolved: 50%**

Round 2 added real, named costs: travel for relationship-building, a data protection program, an MVP build, and implicitly a hire or partner to cover capacity (§12). The Base-case illustrative revenue (RM120,000-300,000/year) is never checked against these costs to show the venture is even worth running at that scale. A credible plan states, even roughly: at Base-case revenue, are we profitable, breakeven, or still burning — and for how long. Without this, the scenario table added in Round 2 is descriptive, not decision-useful.

## Objection 17 — No one is named as physically present in Malaysia, and every tool/channel in this plan is Bangladesh-centric

**Probability of Rejection if Unresolved: 40%**

The existing site is Bengali-first, the WhatsApp numbers referenced throughout are Bangladesh-registered, and the plan's own §2.4/§12 describe "meetings in Dhaka and Kuala Lumpur" without saying who does the Kuala Lumpur half or how. Building real ministry and RL relationships in Malaysia plausibly requires actual physical presence and local credibility there, not remote outreach from Dhaka. The plan should name how the Malaysia-side presence gets solved: a co-founder/partner based in Malaysia, a paid local advisor/fixer relationship, or accepting a slower timeline that accounts for founder travel constraints.
