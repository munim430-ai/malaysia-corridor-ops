## ATTACK 2: "2-3 lakh leaves profit"

### Original Assumption
> Charging workers 2-3 lakh BDT all-in (vs market's 6-7 lakh) leaves enough margin.

### Evidence Found

**Malaysia-side costs (per worker):**
- Levy: RM1,850/yr (manufacturing/construction/services) or RM640 (plantation/agriculture)
- FWCMS fee: RM215 (Bestinet processing)
- VDR (Visa Dengan Rujukan): ~RM250 (estimated)
- FOMEMA medical: ~RM180-250
- Insurance (FWCS + SKHPPA): ~RM500/yr
- Airfare (KL-Dhaka-KL): ~RM1,000-1,200
- Security bond: ~RM500 (refundable, but must be paid upfront)
- Total MY side: ~RM4,200-4,750 (BDT ~71,000-81,000)

**Bangladesh-side costs (per worker):**
- BMET clearance/smart card: ~BDT 10,000-15,000
- Medical (approved center): ~BDT 8,000-12,000
- PDOT training: ~BDT 5,000-8,000
- Passport: ~BDT 5,000
- Police clearance: ~BDT 1,000
- Welfare Board fee: ~BDT 5,000-7,000
- Village sourcing/agent commission: ~BDT 20,000-30,000
- Bank guarantee/insurance: ~BDT 5,000
- Total BD side: ~BDT 59,000-83,000

**Total minimum cost floor:** ~BDT 130,000-164,000 (RM 7,600-9,600)

**At 2.5 lakh BDT (RM14,500):** Gross margin = BDT 86,000-120,000 (RM5,000-7,000) per worker
**At 3.0 lakh BDT (RM17,400):** Gross margin = BDT 136,000-170,000 (RM8,000-10,000) per worker

**At market rate 6.5 lakh BDT (RM37,700):** Gross margin = BDT 486,000-520,000 (RM28,000-30,000) per worker

This means:
- At 2.5 lakh: ~51-73% gross margin on costs (thin for the risk involved)
- At 3.0 lakh: ~83-104% gross margin (sustainable)
- At 6.5 lakh (market): ~296-400% gross margin (current exploit pricing)

### Verdict: **REVISE**

The profit analysis assumed our costs are similar to incumbents'. They're not:
- Incumbents have inflated costs (village dalal commissions, government kickbacks, multiple agent layers).
- Our cost advantage comes from eliminating those layers, not from lower processing costs.
- **2.5 lakh is viable** at volume but margins are thin for a startup.
- **3.0 lakh is safer** and still 54% below market — compelling value proposition.

### Corrected Statement
> "3.0 lakh BDT (RM17,400) is the realistic minimum price with sustainable margin. 2.5 lakh can work at scale (500+ workers/yr) but as a launch price, it risks negative margin from unforeseen costs. Launch at 3.0-3.5 lakh, target 2.5 lakh at scale."

## ATTACK 9: "We can operate without a recruiting license (RL)"

### Original Assumption
> A consultancy or information service model doesn't require a BMET Recruiting License.

### Evidence Found
- **Bangladesh Overseas Employment Act 2013:** Anyone recruiting workers for overseas employment requires a BMET license. Section 4 prohibits unlicensed recruitment. Source: [BMET](https://bmet.gov.bd/)
- **Consultancy model:** A consultancy advising on overseas employment WITHOUT actually processing worker deployment may not need an RL. But the line is blurry.
- **Malaysia-side entity (SSM/KDN):** Setting up a Malaysian company + KDN agency license + BD sourcing arm is a viable structure.
- **Umbrella deal:** Using an existing RL holder's license with a revenue share agreement is common practice in Bangladesh. 30-50% to the RL holder is typical.
- **Risk:** Unlicensed recruitment carries criminal penalties under the Emigration Ordinance 1982.

### Verdict: **CONFIRM** (with caveats)

You CAN operate without your own RL in the following structures:
1. **Consultancy service:** Advise, inform, register interest — NO recruiting. But you cannot deploy workers.
2. **Umbrella RL:** Full operations under a licensed RL holder's license. Legal but gives the RL holder leverage.
3. **Malaysia entity:** Register SSM company in Malaysia + KDN agency license. Sourcing arm in BD can operate as "overseas principal" rather than "recruiting agency."
4. **Hybrid (recommended):** Malaysia company handles employer relationship, umbrella RL partner handles BD processing. Move toward own RL when scale justifies the cost (estimated ~BDT 5 lakh + bank guarantee).

### Corrected Statement
> "You can start legally WITHOUT your own BMET RL through: (1) information service only (no recruiting), (2) umbrella agreement with RL holder (30-50% rev share), or (3) Malaysia entity + BD sourcing arm. However, to deploy workers at scale legally, an RL is eventually required. Budget 6 months and ~BDT 5-8 lakh for RL acquisition."

## ATTACK 13: [NEW FAILURE MODE] — Large Employers Locked with Incumbents

### The Failure Mode
The top target employers (Top Glove, Inari, FGV, Sime Darby) already have established relationships with major BAIRA agencies and may be unwilling to switch to a new, unproven partner.

### Evidence
- Malaysian employers in plantation and manufacturing sectors have used the same agency partners for 5-10+ years
- Switching costs: new SOPs, trust-building, compliance documentation
- Large employers (10,000+ foreign workers) need high-volume capacity — new agencies can't initially handle 1,000+ workers/month
- However: NEW factories, NEW compliance requirements (CBP/RBA), and NEW sectors may create openings

### Verdict: **CONFIRMED**

### Risk Level: HIGH (7/10)

### Mitigation
- **Don't fight for the big fish first.** Target:
  1. SMEs (100-500 workers) who can't get attention from big agencies
  2. New factories/expansions announced in 2025-2026 (no existing relationship)
  3. Companies with CBP/RBA compliance pressure (need clean channel)
  4. Companies in sectors where incumbents have poor service reputation
- Once we have 6+ months of successful SME deployments with clean documentation, approach large employers with case studies
- Compliance-pressured companies are MORE likely to switch to a documented, RBA-aligned channel even if it means changing partners
