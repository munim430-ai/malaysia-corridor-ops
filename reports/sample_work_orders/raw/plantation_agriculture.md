# Sample Demand Letters / Job Orders — Plantation & Agriculture Sectors (Bangladesh → Malaysia)

Research conducted 2026-07-20. Goal: locate real, historical demand letters / job orders /
manpower requisition letters from Malaysian employers in the Plantation (palm oil) and
Agriculture (poultry, aquaculture, food processing, crop farming) sectors, dated before
June 2024, showing business-document content (employer, sector, worker count, wages,
contract terms) — not individual worker personal-data files.

---

## CCK Farm (Cameron Highlands, Pahang) — Agriculture / Crop Farming

- **Source URL:** https://www.scribd.com/document/722057429/DEMAND-LETTER-CCK-FARM-3
  (title: "Demand Letter for Foreign Workers Recruitment"; companion/duplicate version at
  https://www.scribd.com/document/722057428/CCK-BL-2, title "CCK BL 2" — same employer,
  same date, near-identical letter with slightly different allowance figures, likely two
  drafts/versions of the same requisition batch)
- **Employer:** CCK Farm (company reg. no. IP0255555-W), No.11 Jalan Farmland, Kampung Raja,
  39010 Cameron Highlands, Pahang, Malaysia. Tel: 05-4983425 / 013-529 2425.
- **Recipient / Bangladesh recruiting agency:** Navira Limited, 3 Shahid Tajuddin Ahmed
  Sorani, 3rd Floor, Moghbazar, Dhaka 1217, Bangladesh — Recruiting Licence No. RL-712.
- **Sector:** Agriculture (Cameron Highlands is Malaysia's main highland vegetable/crop
  farming district; job description reads "Crops Worker" — this is general crop/vegetable
  farm work, not poultry/aquaculture, but squarely in the "Agriculture" bucket).
- **Date:** 5 February 2023 (before the June 2024 corridor closure).
- **Representative excerpt (verbatim, version 722057429):**

  > CCK FARM (IP0255555-W)

  >     borne by employer upon completion of contract.
  > 17. Yearly Medical: arranged and paid by employer as required by Immigration Department.
  > 18. Restriction: Employee shall not change employment during contract period nor carry
  >     on business without written permission... [standard termination/conduct clauses follow]

- **Companion version (722057428 / "CCK BL 2"), same date, shows a second worked salary
  example:** base RM 1,500 (same formula), Attendance RM 50, Overtime RM 674.70, Meal
  Allowance RM 78, **Total Monthly Salary RM 2,302.70** — otherwise identical boilerplate
  terms, same employer, same recipient (Navira Limited), same date. Likely two draft/batch
  versions of the same requisition.

- **Notes:** Full letter text was recoverable because Scribd had embedded an OCR/HTML text
  layer for this particular document (most other Scribd docs found were image-only scans

### Ali Overseas Pvt. Ltd. template — blank/generic, not Bangladesh
- **Source URL:** https://www.scribd.com/document/716881808/Demand-Letter-for-Ali-Overesas-MALAYSIA
- A blank fill-in-the-blank demand letter template addressed to the Embassy of Nepal in

### BOESL / Bangladesh government generic guidance (not a filled sample)
- **Source URL:** https://www.academia.edu/30665209/RECRUITMENT_PROCEDURES_Placement_of_Demand_Letter_Power_of_Attorney
  (mirrors the official BOESL PDF at
  https://boesl.portal.gov.bd/.../Demand%20Letter.pdf, which returned HTTP 503 on repeated
  fetch attempts)
- Describes the demand letter *procedure* generically: "Employers willing to use services
  for employment of Bangladeshi people may establish direct contact with BOESL or send a
  demand letter for workers to the nearest Bangladesh Mission... it is necessary to state
  the type of workers required, nature of work, number of workers, wages/salary,
  qualifications and experience and other terms and conditions of service and benefits the
  employer would like to offer." No specific employer, sector, or filled example — this is
  process documentation, not a sample letter.
- A similar generic (blank) template was found at
  https://www.bangladesh-humanresources.com/demand_letter.php — a manpower demand table
  template with no Malaysia- or plantation-specific content.
- A "Sample Demand Letter" PDF from Bangladesh's Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  (file.mofa.gov.bd) was located via search but the server returned HTTP 503 / timeout on
  every fetch attempt (direct, WebFetch, and proxy), so its content could not be verified.

### IOI Group Plantation — internal policy document, not a demand letter
- **Source URL:** https://www.ioigroup.com/content/s/pdf/foreign%20workers%20recruitment%20guideline%20procedure.pdf
- This is IOI Plantation's own "Foreign Workers Recruitment Guideline & Procedure"
  (October 2017, revised July 2018), a genuine, publicly-hosted PDF confirming IOI recruits
  from Bangladesh among other source countries ("v. Bangladesh") for its plantation
  operations, and describing its internal requisition workflow (Operating Centre sends
  requisition to HR Department, which seeks Director approval, then engages recruitment
  agencies). It is **not** a demand letter/job order itself — no specific worker counts,
  wages, or sector-line-item content — so it does not satisfy the "demand letter" format
  requirement, but is included as corroborating evidence that major Malaysian palm oil
  companies did source Bangladeshi labour through formal agency-mediated requisition
  processes before the 2024 freeze.

## Not found

- **No palm oil plantation-specific demand letter with full readable text** (naming a real
  estate/plantation company, worker count, and palm-oil-specific job category such as
  "harvester," "field worker," "FFB collector") was located despite many targeted queries.
  Multiple sources confirm plantation-sector demand letters exist and are submitted in bulk
  (e.g. news reports of "7,500 applications... palm oil plantation sector," Bursa-listed
  planters, FELDA/FGV/Sime Darby Plantation coverage of Bangladeshi worker recruitment), but
  no publicly viewable example document (Scribd, Docplayer, PDFCoffee, SlideShare,
  Academia.edu, or plain web) could be found and read.
- **No poultry-sector or aquaculture-sector demand letter** was found. Searches for "CCK
  Farm" (a real Malaysian poultry/livestock group, CCK Consolidated Holdings Berhad, based
  in Sarawak) turned up only the Cameron Highlands vegetable-farm document above, which
  appears to be an unrelated smaller operation of similar name rather than the listed
  poultry conglomerate — could not confirm any corporate relationship.
- **No food-processing sector demand letter** was found.
- Scribd hosts several other "Demand Letter" titled documents (e.g. document IDs
  239660950/319093902 "2.1 Demand Letter" — a Balakong, Selangor manufacturing-sector
  letter for factory general workers, and 445012847/306066052 "Demand Letter and
  Employment Letter" — Exelite Resources Sdn Bhd recruiting **Myanmar** workers for
  factory/production roles) but these are wrong country (not Bangladesh) or wrong sector
  (manufacturing), so they were excluded.

## Search process

**Tools used:** WebSearch (primary), WebFetch (failed on nearly all Scribd URLs — Scribd
serves a Cloudflare "Client Challenge" / JS-only shell to both WebFetch and raw curl), and
as a workaround, the public `r.jina.ai` reader proxy (`https://r.jina.ai/<url>`) fetched
via Bash/curl, which successfully rendered several Scribd pages' free-preview text
(including embedded OCR text layers where Scribd had one). Direct PDF downloads (curl +
`pdftotext`) worked for the IOI Group PDF but failed for BOESL and the Bangladesh MOFA PDF
(connection/503 errors on every attempt, including via the jina proxy).

**Queries tried (18 total), roughly in order:**
1. `site:scribd.com "demand letter" Malaysia plantation worker Bangladesh`
2. `site:scribd.com "job order" Malaysia palm oil estate worker`
3. `"manpower requisition" Malaysia plantation Bangladesh BMET`
4. `"demand letter for recruitment of workers" Malaysia estate OR plantation`
5. `"Al-Rabeta" demand letter Malaysia workers Bangladesh sector plantation OR agriculture OR poultry`
6. `site:docplayer.net "demand letter" Malaysia Bangladesh worker`
7. `site:pdfcoffee.com "demand letter" Malaysia worker Bangladesh`
8. `site:slideshare.net "demand letter" Malaysia recruitment Bangladesh worker`

**What worked:** Scribd's own search-result "AI-enhanced description" snippets (returned
inline by WebSearch, without even opening the page) were often the single richest source of
content — several genuine document summaries were extracted this way. The `r.jina.ai` proxy
then let some individual Scribd documents' free-preview text be read directly when Scribd
had an OCR/text layer (worked for CCK Farm's two documents and Sampan Maju's letter; failed
— image-only — for the Al-Rabeta documents).

**What didn't work:** Direct WebFetch and curl against Scribd (blocked by Cloudflare
challenge every time). Docplayer, PDFCoffee, and SlideShare site-restricted searches
returned no Malaysia/Bangladesh plantation-relevant results at all — those platforms don't
appear to host this specific document type for this corridor. Bengali and Malay-language
queries surfaced Bangladeshi and Malaysian news coverage of the plantation-sector
recruitment reopening process (useful context, confirms real recruitment volumes and that a
"চাহিদাপত্র" / demand-letter attestation process exists) but no actual letter documents.
Government PDF sources (BOESL, Bangladesh MOFA, Bangladesh High Commission KL) were either
unreachable (503/timeout) or, when reached, contained only generic undated templates rather
than real filled examples.
