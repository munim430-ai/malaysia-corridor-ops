# Sample Demand Letters / Job Orders — Services, Retail, Hospitality (Malaysia–Bangladesh corridor)

**Research date:** 2026-07-20
**Target:** Real demand letters / job orders from Malaysian employers in Services, Retail, or Hospitality (hotels/resorts), dated before June 2024.

## Summary of outcome

After an extensive multi-query search (English, Malay, and Bengali terms; site-restricted and open searches across Scribd, Docplayer, PDFCoffee, Academia.edu, SlideShare, Studocu, and general news/government sources), I was **not able to find a genuine, completed, employer-specific demand letter for a named Malaysian company in the Services, Retail, or Hospitality (hotel/resort) sector**. The real demand letters that are publicly viewable online for the Bangladesh→Malaysia corridor are overwhelmingly for **manufacturing, construction, and generic "general worker"** categories — this matches what secondary sources say about the corridor's actual composition (the bulk of BMET-processed Bangladeshi labour migration to Malaysia in 2022–2024 was manufacturing/plantation/construction, not hospitality/retail).

I did find several **genuine, real-world demand letter documents** (useful as format/content references even though most are outside the three target sectors) and one **genuine blank agency template whose job-category list spans into services-type roles** (cleaner, security guard). These are recorded below, each labeled with its actual sector so it's clear none is a confirmed hospitality/retail/services example with a named employer.

- **Sector:** General construction (NOT services/retail/hospitality — included only as a genuine, dated, real-employer example of the format)
- **Employer:** Sampan Maju Enterprise, A-4-16, Jalan Helang, Sungai Dua 11700, Gelugor, Pulau Pinang, Malaysia
- **Excerpt/details (from preview):** Requests recruitment of 3,000 workers from Bangladesh, Nepal, and Pakistan for general construction work. Restricts marriage to Malaysian citizens and political/trade-union activity.

### 4. Agensi Pekerjaan OSM Sdn Bhd — Blank recruitment-agency template (job categories include services-type roles)
- **Source URLs:** https://www.scribd.com/document/319093902/2-1-Demand-Letter and a duplicate/related upload at https://www.scribd.com/document/239660950/2-1-Demand-Letter (this second copy, uploaded by "Ali Overseas Nepal," is unfilled with sector checkboxes for **"Manufacturing, Mining and Quarry, Services and Construction"**)
- **Date:** Template; one copy shows an unfilled placeholder "00/00/2022"
- **Sector:** Generic template — NOT employer- or sector-specific, but the job-category list explicitly includes services-adjacent roles
- **Company:** Agensi Pekerjaan OSM Sdn. Bhd. (962428-H), 56G, Jalan PDR 5, Kawasan Perniagaan Desa Ria, 43300 Balakong, Selangor Darul Ehsan, Malaysia — a real, named, licensed Malaysian recruitment agency
- **Excerpt (verbatim fragments from the preview):**
  > "RE: DEMAND LETTER FOR RECRUITMENT OF WORKERS FROM 'COUNTRY' (MALE/FEMALE)"
  > "Basic minimum salary should not be less than: RM 34.62 / day * 8 hours * 26 days = RM 900.00 for General Workers"
  > Job categories offered in the template: General Worker / Machine Operator / **Cleaner** / **Private Security Guard**
  > "Working Hours: 8 hours/day; 48 hours/week." Overtime per Malaysian Labour Law (1.5x normal, 2.0x Sunday, 3.0x public holiday).
  > "The Employer shall provide the Worker with decent accommodation with basic amenities that meets the stipulated minimum standards on safety and health," with a possible RM50/month accommodation deduction.
  > Contract duration shown as "two (2) years from the date of arrival of worker" in one version, 3 years in another.
- **Why it's included:** This is the closest genuine document to the target — a real, named, licensed Malaysian agency's own demand-letter template that explicitly lists "Services" as a sector option and includes Cleaner/Security Guard as recruitable job categories. However, it is a **blank template**, not a completed letter for a specific hospitality/retail employer, so it does not fully satisfy the brief.

## Off-target documents found and excluded

- A Kuwait-specific "Demand Letter Attestation File" (https://www.scribd.com/document/558091852/Demand-Letter-Attestation-File-18-1) does contain genuine hospitality job categories (Chef, Waiter, Kitchen Helper) with named employers (Top International Catering Co., Las Palmas Restaurant Co.) and wages in Kuwaiti Dinars — but it is a **Nepal→Kuwait** corridor document from 2011, not Malaysia, so it was excluded per the brief.
- A generic "Demand Letter for Ali Overseas MALAYSIA" (https://www.scribd.com/document/716881808/Demand-Letter-for-Ali-Overesas-MALAYSIA) is a blank template with sector field listing "Manufacturing, Mining and Quarry, Services and Construction" as checkbox options but no employer filled in and no sector selected.
- News coverage of the 2023–2024 Bangladesh–Malaysia recruitment-fraud scandal (e.g., 33 workers suing Meranti Binamas Sdn Bhd, reported by The Daily Star / andyjhall.org / Business & Human Rights Resource Centre) confirms that "forged demand letters and fake company names" were central to that scam, and that the scam mostly targeted **construction** jobs — reinforcing that services/retail/hospitality demand letters are less prevalent/visible in the public record for this corridor.

What worked technically: Scribd pages could not be read directly via WebFetch (they returned a client-side loading error), but routing the same URL through the `r.jina.ai` reader proxy (e.g., `https://r.jina.ai/https://www.scribd.com/document/...`) successfully extracted preview text for most Scribd documents. This is how documents #1–#4 above were actually read.

What didn't work: Docplayer.net appears unreachable from this environment (proxy rejected the CONNECT). Two Bangladesh-government PDF hosts (`boesl.portal.gov.bd`, `file.mofa.gov.bd`) consistently failed — 503/422 via WebFetch and r.jina.ai, and SSL-certificate / connection-reset errors via direct `curl` (even using the environment's provided CA bundle) — so their content could not be verified. PDFCoffee, Academia.edu, SlideShare, and Studocu searches for this specific corridor+sector combination returned no genuinely relevant hits (mostly generic legal demand-letter templates from unrelated jurisdictions, or off-topic hospitality-dispute letters).

## Not found

- **No genuine, completed demand letter/job order from a named Malaysian employer in Hospitality (hotel/resort)**, dated pre-June 2024, was located.
- **No genuine, completed demand letter/job order from a named Malaysian employer in Retail**, dated pre-June 2024, was located.
- **No genuine, completed demand letter/job order from a named Malaysian employer in general Services** (cleaning company, security company, facilities management, etc. as the employer itself — as opposed to a generic agency template that merely lists "Cleaner" as a job category), dated pre-June 2024, was located.
- The Bangladesh MOFA and BOESL official demand-letter-format PDFs (which might have contained a services/hospitality sample or a generic template usable as a stand-in) could not be retrieved due to persistent server/connectivity errors from this environment.
- A Bangladesh High Commission (Kuala Lumpur) "Demand Letter" webpage (https://www.bdhckl.gov.bd/demand-letter/) exists but its content, when fetched, was only site navigation — no actual document or sample text was present at that URL.

Given the search effort (25 distinct queries across three languages and multiple document-hosting platforms), I'm reporting this as a genuine gap rather than fabricating a services/retail/hospitality example. The one qualifying document found — the Agensi Pekerjaan OSM Sdn Bhd blank template — is real, named, and touches services-type job categories, but is explicitly flagged above as not being a completed, sector-specific, named-employer letter.
