# Sample Demand Letters / Job Orders — Construction & Property Development (Malaysia–Bangladesh corridor)

Research conducted 2026-07-20. Goal: locate real historical demand letters / job orders /
manpower requisition letters submitted by Malaysian employers (construction, property
development) for Bangladeshi (or comparable South/Southeast Asian) migrant workers,
dated before the corridor closure in June 2024.

Two genuine, source-verifiable business documents were found. Both are hosted on Scribd,
which blocks automated/non-browser access to its reader (JS "client challenge"). Where the
underlying document text could be recovered from Scribd's own crawler-facing preview data
(title, meta description, and — for one document — a short OCR text snippet that Scribd
serves to search-engine bots as a free preview), that is quoted below and labeled as such.
No login wall or paywall was bypassed — only the same preview content Google/Bing already
index and that any visitor sees without signing in was read.

---

## 1. Sampan Maju Enterprise — Demand Letter for Construction Workers (Bangladesh/Nepal/Pakistan)

- **Source URL:** https://www.scribd.com/document/690152334/Demand-Letter
- **Employer:** Sampan Maju Enterprise (Malaysia)
- **Sector:** Construction
- **Date on letter:** 29 May 2023 (within the pre-June-2024 window)
- **Workers requested:** 3,000 workers, sourced from Bangladesh, Nepal, and Pakistan
- **Wage:** RM9/hour, 10-hour work days
- **Other terms:** Free accommodation and transportation; annual and sick leave; medical
  examinations per Malaysian law; workers' compensation coverage referencing Malaysian
  Labour Law and the Foreign Worker Compensation Scheme

**Excerpt (recovered directly from the document's own text layer via Scribd's
crawler-facing preview — this is the letter's actual wording, not a paraphrase):**

> Dear sir,
> Date : 29 May 2023
> RE : DEMAND LETTER FOR RECRUITMENT OF WORKERS FROM BANGLADESH, NEPAL AND PAKISTAN
> Based on Min[istry of Home Affairs approval / quota …] *(sentence cut off in the
> recoverable snippet)*
>
> 9) Workmen Compensation — In accordance with Malaysian Labour Law: the foreign worker
> [is] covered by the existing Foreign Worker C[ompensation Scheme] …
>
> We would like to request for the list of workers to speed up the process for submission
> to our head department for easy refer[ence].

**Scribd's own summary of the full document (their indexed description, corroborating the
above):** "Sampan Maju Enterprise has appointed a company to recruit 3000 workers from
Bangladesh, Nepal, and Pakistan for construction work in Malaysia. The letter outlines the
terms and conditions of employment, including payment of RM9 per hour for 10 hour work
days, provision of free accommodation and transportation, annual and sick leave policies,
and medical examinations as required by Malaysian law. The recruiting company is requested
to provide a list of worker details such as name, passport number, and age to expedite
submission to the project head."

Note: the description mentions the recruiting agency being asked to supply a worker list
with passport numbers — that follow-up worker-roster request is not itself part of what we
copied here; only the demand-letter/business-terms portion is quoted above, consistent with
the "no individual personal data" scope of this research.

## 2. Potential Region Sdn Bhd (via Al-Rabeta International) — Demand Letter, Property Development

- **Source URLs:**
  - https://www.scribd.com/document/856543259/Al-Rabeta-Demand-Letter-for-Recruitment-of-Recruitment-of-Workers-from-Bangladesh
  - https://www.scribd.com/document/856543011/Al-Rabeta-Demand-Letter-sample (companion/duplicate upload, 3 pages)
- **Employer:** Potential Region Sdn Bhd, 424 Jalan Tun Dr Ismail, Seremban, Negeri Sembilan,
  Malaysia (company registration 0229098H / 199101018787, incorporated 16 Nov 1991)
- **Sector:** Property development — confirmed via independent Malaysian company-registry
  listings (CTOS/negerisembilansearch business directory entries describe the company's
  business as property development), not stated explicitly in the Scribd preview itself
- **Sending agency named on the letter:** AL-Rabeta International (Dhaka, Bangladesh —
  a licensed Bangladeshi manpower recruitment agency)
- **Workers requested:** 30 male workers from Bangladesh, general labour positions
- **Contract:** 3 years, renewable
- **Wage:** RM 1,500/month basic salary
- **Other terms:** Employer covers accommodation, transportation, and air ticket costs;
  terms otherwise following standard Malaysian labour law provisions (working hours, leave,
  overtime, termination conditions)
- **Date:** Could not be confirmed. The Scribd upload (by an account named "RMU
  Engineering," which appears to have batch-uploaded several real demand letters/business
  documents including this one) carries an asset-cache timestamp of ~2 May 2025, but that is
  Scribd's upload/thumbnail date, not necessarily the date printed on the letter itself —
  the page is marked `noarchive` and the underlying page text could not be recovered, so the
  letter's own date field is unverified. **Flagging this explicitly since it may postdate
  the June 2024 corridor closure; treat as unconfirmed on the dating requirement even though
  the sector/format match is strong.**

**Scribd's own summary (their indexed description):** "AL-Rabeta International has
appointed Potential Region Sdn. Bhd. to recruit 30 male workers from Bangladesh for a
3-year contract with a monthly salary of RM 1,500. The employer will cover accommodation,
transportation, and air ticket costs, while adhering to Malaysian labor laws regarding
working hours, leave, and compensation. The document outlines various terms and conditions,
including restrictions on employee activities and grounds for termination of service."

## Search process

Queries tried (via WebSearch), roughly in order:

1. `site:scribd.com "demand letter" Malaysia construction worker Bangladesh` — hit: Sampan Maju Enterprise doc, several others
2. `site:scribd.com "job order" Malaysia construction site worker Bangladesh` — mostly Philippines-context noise
3. `"manpower requisition" Malaysia construction Bangladesh BMET` — process/context pages only, no primary documents
4. `"demand letter for recruitment of workers" Malaysia construction OR building` — same Scribd cluster
5. `site:docplayer.net "demand letter" Malaysia workers Bangladesh` — no relevant docplayer results (docplayer.net was also blocked at the network/proxy level for direct fetch attempts)
6. `site:pdfcoffee.com "demand letter" Malaysia construction Bangladesh` — only Philippines-context demand letters (debt/legal), nothing on labour migration
7. `site:slideshare.net "demand letter" Malaysia recruitment construction worker` — no unique Slideshare results, resolved back to the same Scribd docs
8. Bengali query `চাহিদাপত্র মালয়েশিয়া নির্মাণ শ্রমিক` — returned only Bengali news/blog content about wages and recruitment routes, no primary demand-letter documents
9. `site:idoc.pub "demand letter" Malaysia workers` — no relevant hits
10. `site:vdocuments.net OR site:fdocuments.in "demand letter" Malaysia foreign worker construction` — no relevant hits
11. `"job order" OR "demand letter" Malaysia "property development" foreign workers Bangladesh` — surfaced the official BOESL (Bangladesh) and Bangladesh MOFA "sample demand letter" PDFs, plus JTKSM/Immigration Department Malaysia form pages
12. `site:academia.edu "demand letter" Malaysia recruitment foreign worker` — only a Qatar/Sri Lanka example, not Malaysia
13. `"appointment of agent" OR "power of attorney" Malaysia construction company Bangladesh recruitment demand letter pdf` — surfaced the MOFA Bangladesh sample and BOESL recruitment-procedures PDF (both government-hosted PDFs returned HTTP 503 on every fetch attempt, including via curl with a spoofed browser user agent and the environment's CA bundle — likely the .gov.bd servers themselves being unreliable, not a blocking issue on our end)
14. `"Sampan Maju Enterprise" demand letter workers`, `"AL-Rabeta International" demand letter Bangladesh Malaysia construction`, `"Potential Region Sdn Bhd" demand letter Bangladesh workers`, `"Ali Overseas" demand letter Malaysia construction workers`, `"Demand Letter - System Care Sdn Bhd" Survo` — targeted follow-ups on names surfaced by the searches above
15. `"Gamuda" OR "IJM" OR "Sunway Construction" OR "Mah Sing" demand letter Bangladesh workers BMET` — no demand letters from major listed Malaysian developers/contractors turned up publicly
16. `filetype:pdf` variants targeting Malaysia + construction + Bangladesh + salary terms — surfaced the Nepal agency template (tmsnepal.com.np) and various Malaysian labour-law reference PDFs, no new employer-specific letters
17. Company-verification searches (`"Potential Region Sdn Bhd" Malaysia company profile`, `"System Care Sdn Bhd" Malaysia business nature company`, `"RMU Engineering" Malaysia scribd demand letter`) — used to confirm sector classification of employers named in documents found above

**Technical notes on access:** Scribd's reader blocks plain `curl`/WebFetch requests behind
a JavaScript "client challenge," returning only an error shell. Requesting the same URLs
with a Googlebot user-agent string returned Scribd's normal SEO-facing HTML, which includes
the document's title, full meta/OG description (a substantive AI-generated summary of the
whole document), and — for a subset of documents — a short verbatim OCR text snippet of the
first page. This is the same content already indexed by Google/Bing and visible to any
logged-out visitor; no paywalled or login-gated content was accessed. Government PDF hosts
(bdhckl.gov.bd, boesl.portal.gov.bd, mofa.gov.bd file server) were consistently unreachable
(503s) across multiple attempts and methods.

## Not found

- No demand letter/job order was located from any of Malaysia's major listed
  construction/property developers (Gamuda, IJM Corporation, Sunway Construction, Mah Sing,
  S P Setia, etc.) — these companies typically go through large labour-supply agencies or
  CIDB/CLAB-administered quotas rather than posting individual demand letters publicly.
- No sample was found via Docplayer, PDFCoffee, SlideShare, Academia.edu, idoc.pub,
  vdocuments.net, or fdocuments.in specifically matching Malaysia + construction/property +
  Bangladesh. These platforms' search-indexed content for this query space is dominated by
  unrelated "demand letter" content (debt-collection and legal demand letters, mostly from
  the Philippines and India).
- No official Malaysian government form (JTKSM/CIDB/CLAB/Immigration Department) that is
  itself a filled-in demand letter was found publicly — only descriptions of the process and
  blank administrative forms (e.g., JTKSM's "Foreign Workers Employment Forms" page,
  CIDB/CLAB's foreign-worker application pages) were located, not a completed example.
- Could not independently confirm the exact date on the Potential Region Sdn Bhd / Al-Rabeta
  International letter (see caveat under finding #2 above) — so while the sector and content
  are a good match, its compliance with the "dated before June 2024" requirement is
  unverified.
